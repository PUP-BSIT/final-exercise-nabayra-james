Personal Journal

When I was in Grade 10, I was contemplating which track or strand to choose when I reached Grade 11 or entered senior high school. Based on my National Achievement Test (NAT) results, I scored high in the HUMSS strand, which focuses more on social interaction and public speaking— a result that resonated with my existing skills. However, I carefully considered my family, myself, and various factors before deciding on a strand. I sought advice from relatives, those close to me, and people in my surroundings. I thought not only about where I excelled but also about securing a future for my loved ones and ensuring my personal satisfaction. I thought deeply about the chosen strand because I wanted the course I would take in senior high school to align with my college course, providing me with a background in the field. That led me to choose the TVL track with a focus on ICT (Information and Communications Technology). There were many reasons behind this decision. As I assessed myself, I felt excited and genuinely interested in learning from our teachers. I tracked my progress from Grade 11 to Grade 12. While I may not have been exceptional, I kept up, and that was the most crucial aspect. In Grade 11, I ranked fourth in our strand, and in Grade 12, I rebounded to become the top student in our strand. It's amusing to think that I excelled in the chosen strand and even managed to help classmates despite facing challenges. This led me to exclaim, "I can actually do this, I can handle college."

Entering college during a pandemic, I did not expect to pass the entrance exam at PUP-T as one of the BSIT students. I pushed for it because it was my dream course, and the school I entered was reputable. I was taken aback when I saw the knowledge level of my classmates. I struggled to keep up because it seemed like they already had advanced knowledge about our course. Despite being the top-performing student in Grade 12, my knowledge was insufficient. The college environment was vastly different, and I had to help myself rise to the challenges posed by our professors. The first year, particularly the first semester, was extremely tough for me because I was still trying to find my way. It felt like I had no knowledge of what was happening around me. I felt small because I had limited knowledge of coding in various programming languages. We faced hardships due to the workloads assigned by one of our professors. But I knew he gave us those tasks to learn early. I felt pressured with each assignment. However, as the second semester passed, I began to notice growth within myself. Then came the vacation, and I contemplated shifting to another course, specifically BSBA-HRM, just to escape the BSIT course. Unfortunately, there were no available slots in other courses, so I fought through the first semester of the second year. I decided to try again in the second semester, even though they said it was not allowed, and I should have shifted during the first semester. But I said, "Whatever, I'll fight until the end." And here I am now, continuing to pursue my "DREAM COURSE."

I have stumbled many times in this course, but I have also fought and persevered. I won't give up until I achieve my aspirations. I want to become an IT Professional someday. I want a role that doesn't involve too much coding because IT is not all about code. I admit I'm not particularly skilled in coding, and up until now, I still feel like I'm lost and fumbling in the dark. But I am here, and I will see this through to the end. I may get tired, but I won't give up.

I want to land a position in good companies or even not-so-good ones, as long as I fulfill my dream so that all the hardships, pressure, and challenges I've experienced, am experiencing, and will experience will be worth it. I can't just give up suddenly. I know there's a reason why destiny did not transfer me to another course despite multiple attempts. I know I am on this path because the Lord knows I am on the right track. I will continue what I started. "Giving up" is not in my vocabulary. Yes, there are times when I feel like letting go because it seems like I'm not good at coding. I don't know much about what is being taught. I feel like a lost child wandering off the path. But I know myself. I know I will do everything to succeed in the field I have chosen. Despite the challenges, I will persevere because I have a dream, and that dream is not just a dream. I will experience it when the time is right. I am here, and I will continue this journey.

I'll revisit this after 5 years, and I know I'll smile at what I wrote here because I've achieved what I needed to do in life. I'm aware that I'll be busy by then, as I can see myself becoming a workaholic person. James is diligent. I envision that you have a beautiful home, a comfortable life, and have become an IT Professional. I know you've gone through a lot, embraced it, and you're here now. You've experienced the life you wanted. That fatigue you'll feel as you read this comes from hard work for yourself and your beloved family. You have many responsibilities for your family because you're the breadwinner now. Remember to take a break! You deserve it! Don't abuse yourself. You'll read this in 2028. I'm doing many things right and striving now to get us to where you are, so don't forget that it's still a long way, but you're already far!
